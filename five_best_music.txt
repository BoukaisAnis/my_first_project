Ludwig van Beethoven – Symphony No. 9 in D minor, Op. 125

Known as the "Choral Symphony," this masterpiece is renowned for its powerful final movement, "Ode to Joy," which has become a symbol of universal brotherhood.

The Beatles – "A Day in the Life"

From the album Sgt. Pepper's Lonely Hearts Club Band, this song showcases the innovative songwriting and studio techniques that revolutionized popular music.

Bob Dylan – "Like a Rolling Stone"

This groundbreaking song is often credited with transforming the landscape of rock and folk music, thanks to its poetic lyrics and Dylan's raw, powerful delivery.

Johann Sebastian Bach – "Brandenburg Concerto No. 3 in G Major, BWV 1048"

A pinnacle of Baroque music, this concerto is celebrated for its intricate and lively interplay between instruments.

Miles Davis – "So What"

From the album Kind of Blue, this track is a cornerstone of jazz music, known for its modal improvisation and Davis's innovative approach to jazz composition and performance.
