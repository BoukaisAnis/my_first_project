To Kill a Mockingbird" by Harper Lee

A profound novel about racial injustice in the Deep South, seen through the eyes of a young girl, Scout Finch.
"1984" by George Orwell

A dystopian novel that explores themes of totalitarianism, surveillance, and individual freedom, set in a future where the state exerts total control over its citizens.
"Moby-Dick" by Herman Melville

An epic tale of obsession and revenge, following Captain Ahab's relentless pursuit of the titular white whale.
"Pride and Prejudice" by Jane Austen

A classic romantic novel that delves into issues of class, marriage, and social expectations in early 19th-century England.
"War and Peace" by Leo Tolstoy

A monumental work of Russian literature that weaves together the lives of multiple characters against the backdrop of the Napoleonic Wars.
